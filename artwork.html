9th Grade Charcoal - "Walk A Mile"

Artist Statement (from 12/13/18): The memory I was drawing was in kindergarten when my friend and I tried to make wishes by blowing on dandelions but we ended up inhaling the seeds which left us coughing and sputtering. It’s one of the few memories I have about kindergarten which makes the memory valuable to me. The dandelion seeds are self-explanatory. The swing-set chains were nearby as we were at recess, but we were standing on the grass when making wishes. When I was in kindergarten, my private school had us wear uniforms which is why I added the plaid fabric.

10th Grade Acrylic Paint - "Work vs Play"

Artist Statement (from 3/5/20): I responded to the prompt of work versus play by creating a representation in my mind of the struggles of getting through work to enjoy play. The dark colored mountain represents the task of completing all the work you have to do to get to play, which is represented by the yellow ribbon. I elevated the ribbon with the wire to make it apparent that even when you’re finished with all the work of the day, play is still out of reach.
